## 1. Understanding the UK-SPEC Competence Framework

The IET uses the **UK Standard for Professional Engineering Competence (UK-SPEC)** to assess registration applicants. These are not just technical checklists‚Äîthey require structured, evidence-based narratives drawn from real-world experience.

The five competence areas (A‚ÄìE) are:

| Code | Area | Description |
|------|------|-------------|
| A | Knowledge & Understanding | Apply engineering principles and theory |
| B | Design & Development | Develop solutions using engineering methods |
| C | Responsibility, Management, Leadership | Lead tasks, teams, and manage resources |
| D | Communication | Interact clearly and effectively with stakeholders |
| E | Professional Commitment | Demonstrate ethics, safety, sustainability, and CPD |

The IET recommends combining **STAR** and **SMART** techniques to clearly present your experience.

## 2. Structuring Your Competence Using STAR & SMART

Use **‚ÄúI‚Äù** statements to emphasize your personal contributions.

- **STAR** helps present a logical, story-based structure
- **SMART** strengthens your goals and outcomes with clarity and precision

### Sample Competence: C2  
**Definition**: *Plan, budget, organise, direct and control tasks, people, and resources.*

This competence is ideal for demonstrating both STAR and SMART due to its focus on measurable planning and results.
